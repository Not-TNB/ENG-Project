A Review of

The Boy in the Striped Pajamas


General Info

Group Members: Clara, Ellen, Jane, Steve, Tristan
Title: The Boy in the Striped Pajamas
Author: John Boyne
Genre: Historical Fiction



Plot

During World War II, Bruno and his family left Berlin to take up residence near the concentration camp where his father had just become commandant. Unhappy and lonely, he wanders out behind his house and finds a Jewish boy of his age named Shmuel. There, formed a forbidden friendship between the son of a Nazi commandant and a young Jewish prisoner at Auschwitz, oblivious to the real nature of their surroundings.

    What we think

    "I like that the book includes historical events and educates us about how sad the Auschwitz events were in an interesting & engaging way, especially the moments where Bruno sneaks out to see Shmuel. Unfortunately, that didn't appply to all the events that happened in the story as I found some of the content too complicated as to boring" -Jane

    "What i like about the book is that the settings are very detailed and that the story relates to reality, in specific the events that occurred during the Holocaust. What i dont like about the book is that it has a sad ending, in contrast to most other books that i have read." -Tristan

    "I love the fact that the book has a lot of themes and revolves around friendship and we learn a lot of moral lessons such as self-honesty, kindness etc. However, I really dislike the parts of the story where it there were disturbing and depressing contents." -Ellen

    "I think that it is a wonderful book since there are lots of characters each with opposing personalities but there are some violent contents in it which made me felt uncomfortable while reading." -Clara

    "In my opinion, what interests me most about this book is its unique storyline and excellent portrayal of the characters' emotions. Set during World War II, the book provides an insightful description of the living conditions and struggles of Jewish people under the Nazi reign, however, there were too many complicated ties to WWII that made some parts too complicated to understand." -Steve
